What is a Penetration Test?

A penetration test is an attack simulation to assess how secure a network is. The information gathered in a penetration test can be vital for a company. Companies can learn about hidden vulnerabilities in their network. Those vulnerabilities, if not resolved, can be catastrophic.

Types of Penetration Testing

Penetration tests are typically done by ethical hackers. There are three main strategies when it comes to penetration testing. Each strategy has strengths and weaknesses.

1. White Box Testing: With white box testers, the organization gives the ethical hacker a full list of the internal structure of the product being tested.

2. Black Box Testing: With black box testers, the ethical hacker has no knowledge of the infrastructure. It is a real-life simulation of what it would look like for outside hackers to try and hack into the organizationâ€™s network.

3. Gray Box Testing: Gray box testers are in the middle of the white box and black box testers. They are given partial knowledge of the system and a few credentials.
